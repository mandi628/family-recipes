Holly Glob Cookies

The tradition of these cookies started in the late 1980s, when Oma (Cathy (Marchenko) Saidat) found the recipe in a Family Circle Magazine. It originally called for shaping the cookies into rings to make wreaths, but this proved too complicated for us, and the cookies ended up being "globs", so we kept it that way. We've continued to make these cookies every year since, and it's not Christmas without them!

  • 1 16-oz package large marshmallows
  • 1/2 cup butter, softened (1 stick)
  • 1-1/2 tsp vanilla
  • 1-1/2 tsp green food coloring
  • 4-1/2 cup cornflakes cereal
  • 1 2.25 oz. package cinnamon red hot candies
  1. In a saucepan, over low heat, melt the butter, marshmallows, vanilla, and food coloring.
  2. Remove from heat and stir in cornflakes.
  3. Spoon onto wax paper and top with red hot candies.

Jumbles Cookies

I originally found this recipe in an M&M cook book for cooking with the "new" M&M minis. The book was from the late 1990's, or early 2000's. I was not able to find information about the book online, and I no longer have the book. So here is what I have from my notes and my memory.

As I recall, the description of the recipe said something along the lines of, "all the goodies with just enough dough to hold them all together."

  • 1 stick butter, softened
  • 1/2 cup sugar
  • 1/4 cup brown sugar
  • 1 egg
  • 1-1/4 cup flour
  • 1/2 tsp baking soda
  • 1 pkg M&M mini baking bits (can be replaced with chocolate chips or 12 ox. of regular M&Ms)
  • 1 cup raisins
  • 1 cup chopped walnuts
  1. Cream butter and sugars with an electric mixer.
  2. Add egg and beat 1 minutes more.
  3. Add flour and baking soda, using mixer at first, then by hand, if needed.
  4. With wooden spoon, stir in M&Ms, rasins, and walnuts.
  5. Spoon onto cookie sheets and bake at 375 for 9-11 minutes.

Kutya with Barley

Cook time: 1 hour 50 minutes

Based on barley, this kutia also includes frozen berries, cherries, and raisins. Regionally, barley-based kutia is made in the Zhytomyr region, where it is enhanced with wine, in the Poltava region where they add uzvar liquid which gives this dish a soup-like texture, while in the Cherkasy region they prefer adding various berries.

Servings: 4

  • 1/2 cup barley
  • 6-7 glasses water
  • 3-4 Tbsp honey or sugar
  • 200-300 g frozen berries (raspberries, strawberries, wild strawberries)
  • 1/2 glass raisins
  • 1 cup pitted cherries, candied or canned
  1. Soak the barley in cold water overnight.
  2. Cook the barley in the water it was soaked in for at least an hour, until the grain softens completely.
  3. Add sugar or honey and the frozen berries and cook over low heat for 30 minutes.
  4. Chop the cherries and add to the kutya, and cook for an additional 10 minutes, until it thickens.
  5. Serve cold.

Source: TasteAtlas Kutia with barley authentic recipe

Mexican Meatball Soup

Cook time: 4 hours in crockpot, 1 hour on stovetop

  • 1 lb frozen Italian meatballs
  • 2/3 cup onion (one whole onion, chopped)
  • 2/3 cup green bell pepper (one whole pepper, chopped)
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 32 oz. beef broth (2 cans or one box)
  • 16-24 oz. jar salsa (choose your own heat)
  • 1 Tbsp chili powder
  • 1/4 tsp ground cumin
  • 1 cup pasta shells or elbow macaroni
  • 2 cups shredded Cheddar cheese
  • 1/4 cup sour cream
  • 1 Tbsp chopped fresh cilantro
  1. Saute onion and green pepper until onions are softened. Place in crockpot with meatballs, garlic, beef broth, salsa, chili powder, and cumin. Stir to combine.
  2. Cover and cook on low 5-7 hours (or high 2-1/2 to 3-1/2 hours).
  3. Add pasta, stir well, cover, and cook on low an additional hour (30 min on high), or until pasta is tender.
  4. Garnish with cheese, sour cream, and cilantro.

Stovetop Instructions

  1. In a stock pot, saute onions and green peppers.
  2. Add meatballs, garlic, beef broth, salsa, and seasonings. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer, covered, on medium-low for 20 minutes.
  3. Add pasta, then recover and simmer an additional 20 minutes, until pasta is cooked.
  4. Serve with cheese, sour cream, and cilantro.

Source: Kelley, Easy Crockpot Albondigas Soup (Mexican Meatball Soup)

Never-Fail Medivnyk

The book has several types of medivnyk, since it is a very popular dessert in Ukraine. This one looked the least intimidating, so I thought I'd give it a try. The individual recipes don't specify how long they need to cure before serving, but the section title says all medivnyks need to cure a few days. Plan to make it ahead of time.

This honey cake is a great use of honey. The Ukrainian word for honey is "med".

  • 1 cup honey
  • 3 cups sifted flour
  • 1 tsp baking soda
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • 1 tsp cinnamon
  • 1/4 tsp salt
  • 1/2 cup strong cool coffee (I used instant)
  • grated rind and juice of 1 orange (I used 2 mandarin oranges, since that's what I had)
  • 1 tsp vanilla
  • 2 Tbsp butter
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 4 eggs, separated
  • 1 cup chopped walnuts
  1. Bring the honey to a boil and then cool it.
  2. Sift the flour with the dry ingredients twice.
  3. Combine the coffee with the grated rind, orange juice, and vanilla.
  4. Cream the butter with the sugar. Mix in the honey.
  5. Beat the egg yolks until light and blend with the honey mixture. Add the flour alternately with the coffee.
  6. Stir in the nuts.
  7. Beat the egg whites until stiff and fold into the batter. Spoon into a buttered baking pan.
  8. Bake in a moderate oven (325F) for about 50 minutes, or until done when tested (toothpick in center comes out clean).
  9. Remove the cake from the pan and place it on a cake rack.

NOTE: This recipe made a lot of batter. I used one bread loaf pan, but it really should have been divided into two. 2023.12.28 ama

Sifting Flour

When a recipe calls for sifted flour, the question always comes up, "Do you measure it before or after sifting?"

According to American's Test Kitchen1, it depends on how the directions are worded.

  • If the instructions say "1 cup sifted flour", for example, sift the flour first, then measure it.
  • If the instructions day "1 cup flour, sifted", then measure the flour first, and sift it after measuring.

When a recipe calls for flour to be "spooned and leveled", you spoon the flour into the measuring cup, then scrape the top off with a butter knife, instead of just scooping it from the container. This allows the flour to unpack and measure more accurately.2

Sourdough Bread

Cook Time: 17-23 hours Serves: 1 loaf

NOTE: The original recipe is from Sourdough Home. I have made notes on my adjustments here.

  • 1 cup active sourdough starter
  • 3 cups all-purpose or bread flour1
  • 1 cup warm water
  • 1-1/2 tsp salt
  1. Combine ingredients in a mixing bowl. Cover and set aside for 12-18 hours.
  2. Uncover and lightly stir dough. Grease your pan and place the dough in the pan. Cover with oiled foil and let sit for up to 4 hours - until double in size.
  3. Place covered pan in cold oven, and set the oven to 425F2 and leave for 50 minutes3.
  4. Remove the cover, and bake 15 minutes more4.
  5. Leave bread in pan to cool for 15 minutes, before turning out onto a cooling rack.
  6. Let cool at least 15 minutes more before slicing.

Sourdough Crepes

Cook time: 11 minutes
Servings: 4

  • 1/2 cup sourdough discard
  • 3 Tbsp melted butter
  • 2 eggs
  • 1/2 cup all-purpose flour
  • 3/4 cup milk
  • 2 Tbsp water
  • 1/4 tsp fine sea salt
  • 3 Tbsp granulated sugar (optional)
  • 1 Tbsp melted butter or fat for cooking
  1. Whisk the ingredients together (except fat for cooking) in a large mixing bowl. Let rest for 1 hour in the fridge.
  2. Heat a lightly greased, non-stick skillet over medium heat.
  3. Pour Â¼ cup of the batter into the skillet and tilt the skillet in a circular motion to distribute the batter into a thin, even layer. Cook until the sides of the batter start to fold back from the skillet and the bottom starts to turn golden brown. About 60-90 seconds.
  4. Gently turn the crepe over using a large spatula. Cook on the second side for 30-60 seconds or until golden brown. Repeat with remaining batter. Keep the crepes warm after they cook by stacking them on a baking sheet in a preheated 350Â°F (176Â°C) oven until ready to serve.

Notes

  • If using whole wheat flour, more liquid may be needed to thin the batter. Add a tablespoon at a time, after it has rested "if needed" to get correct consistency. It should be very thin.
  • Store crepes stacked between layers of parchment or wax paper, wrapped tightly in plastic wrap or freezer bag. Keep in the fridge for up to 7 days or in freezer for up to 2 months.
  • Thaw in fridge before reheating in a skillet over medium heat.

Sourdough Muffins

Makes 6-8 muffins

This recipe is written for blueberries, but you can use any frozen berries you like. If you use strawberries, cut them into quarters.

Wet Ingredients:

  • 1 egg
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1/4 cup vegetable oil (try light olive oil)
  • 1 cup sourdough starter

Dry Ingredients:

  • 1 cup flour (whole wheat or all-purpose white)
  • 3/4 cup frozen blueberries (or other fruit)
  • 1/4 cup white sugar
  • 1/4 tsp salt
  • 1-1/2 tsp baking soda
  1. Preheat oven to 425F.
  2. Combine the Dry Ingredients first.
  3. Stir together the Wet Ingredients.
  4. Stir the dry ingredients into the wet ones.
  5. Place batter into well-greased or lined muffin cups.
  6. Bake for 20 minutes.

Sourdough Pancakes

  • 2 cups flour, spooned and leveled
  • 1 cup sourdough discard
  • 1 tsp baking soda
  • 2 tsp baking powder
  • 3-4 Tbsp sugar
  • 1-3/4 to 2 cups milk
  • 2 large eggs
  • 2 Tbsp vegetable oil
  • 1 tsp salt

If you don't have a full cup of starter, you can half the recipe.

  • 1 cup flour
  • 1/2 cup sourdough discard
  • 1/2 tsp baking soda
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • 2 Tbsp sugar
  • 3/4 to 1 cup milk
  • 1 large egg
  • 1 Tbsp vegetable oil
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  1. Preheat your skillet over medium-low heat for 15 minutes
  2. Combine all your ingredients in a mixing bowl and stir to combine. Lumps are ok.
  3. Melt some butter or oil in your skillet
  4. Add batter to pan and cook until edges are dry and surface is bubbly. Author suggests 1/2 cup of batter per pancake, I like 1/4 cup.
  5. Flip the pancake and cook another 2-3 minutes
  6. Serve immediately.

NOTE: I like pancake batter to be thick, but this was too thick for me. I ended up adding a little more milk, but it will also depend on the thickness of your starter. I also like them a little sweeter, so I added a little extra sugar.

Sourdough Starter

There are starters you can buy. Some are cheap, some are expensive. But it's easy to start your own.

  1. In a clean jar that will hold at least 24 oz., stir together 1/2 cup flour1 and 1/2 cup warm water.2 Stir it well. Cover the jar with a clean cloth and a rubberband, and wait 24 hours.
  2. Place half of your starter in a new jar, and add 1/2 cup flour and 1/2 cup warm water. Stir it together well. Cover again and wait 24 hours. You can throw out the remaining starter from the first jar.
  3. Repeat Step 2.
  4. On the fourth day, you will repeat step 2, but only wait 12 hours before the next feeding. By now, you should notice bubbling in the starter. Continue feeding it every 12 hours.
  5. After about 10 days, the starter should be strong enough to raise bread. You also want it to be relatively thick - it should be the consistency of thick glue. If it's not thick enough, add a little extra flour at the next feeding. If it's too thick, add a little extra water.
  6. Once you have enough activity, you can start using the discard for something like Sourdough Pancakes, Sourdough Crepes, or Sourdough Pizza Crust. You can also measure off two portions of starter and setup two new jars. Keep one out to rise, and put the second one in the fridge. Be sure to feed the backup jar at least once a week.3
  7. You now have a starter ready to make Sourdough Bread, Sourdough English Muffins, or anything else you can think of. Mama also has a worksheet on her computer that allows you to convert any bread recipe into a sourdough recipe.

If you want to find out more about sourdough, you can check out these websites that I go to regularly:
* Sourdough Home

1 Some people swear by whole grain wheat flour or rye flour. I just used plain all-purpose white flour and had good results.
2 Some sources swear that tap water with chlorine will not work to start a starter. I have started with tap water with no problems, and I used spring water from the store. I honestly can't say if one was better than the other. The starter worked both ways.
3 To feed the backup jar, take it out the night before and let it come to room temperature. Do a regular feeding in the morning and leave it to rise. At night, do one more regular feeding, but put it back in the fridge.

Uzvar Liquid

Uzvar is an ancient Ukrainian drink made of mixed dried fruits.

Classic combination is apples, pears and smoked prunes. But of course you can use any fruits that you like. Just avoid the fruits that were soaked in sugar syrup.

Uzvar has natural subtle sweet taste and is pleasant to drink like it is. But feel free to add your favorite sweetener. If you have vegan honey on hand that would be perfect.

Also you can make it spicy by adding cinnamon sticks, star anise and cloves. Spicy Uzvar is the best when served hot.

Cook time: 22 minutes; plus overnight steeping

  • 5 cups mixed dried fruits (apples, pears, prunes, etc.)
  • 3 liters water
  1. Wash your dried fruits if they have dust on them.
  2. Put fruits in a big pot and cover with 3 liters of water. Bring to a boil.
  3. Lower the heat and let it simmer covered for 20 minutes.
  4. Switch off the heat and set aside for at least 8 hours, or overnight.
  5. Strain the liquid and serve. Can be served hot or cold, with your favorite sweetener, if desired.

Source: Uzvar (Dried Fruits Infusion) - Slavic Vegan

Varenyky

This recipe is one that works for us, but there are a ton of variations online, and at least three different types in the Traditional Ukrainian Cookery cookbook. I will add them as time allows so you can find what works for you.

  • 2 cups flour
  • 3/4 cups hot tap water
  • filling
  1. Place flour in a food processor.
  2. Slowly add water, pulsing as you go.
  3. When dough comes together, remove from the food processor. If you're mixing the dough by hand, stir the water into the dough in a bowl until it comes together, then continue.
  4. On a floured board, knead the dough a few times to bring it together.
  5. Cut the dough into 4 parts and cover with a damp towel to keep it from drying out.
  6. Heat a pot of salted water on the stove on medium-high so it's boiling when you're ready.
  7. Take one section of the dough, and roll into a snake. Cut the snake into 12 equal-ish pieces. With a rolling pin, roll out each piece into a circle, flouring as needed to keep it from sticking.
  8. Holding the circle in your palm, lace 2-3 tsp of filling in the center of the circle. Dip your finger into a cup of water and wet the edge of the dough. Fold it over and pinch the edges together, keeping the filling in the middle.
  9. At this point, you have two options. You can make all the varenyky, covering them with a damp towel, then boil them in groups of 6-8. Or, you can place them in the boiling water as you make them.
  10. If you plan to freeze them, boil them for about 2-3 minutes, then place them on a cookie sheet lined with parchment paper, not touching. Place the cookie sheet in the freezer until they are frozen solid (about 2 hours), then put them in Ziploc bags for longer storage. When you're ready to eat them, boil them 3-5 minutes, then toss with melted butter and serve with sour cream.
  11. If you plan to eat them immediately, boil them for about 3-5 minutes, then place them in a bowl with some butter and toss them as you go. Serve with sour cream.

Varenyky & Pyrohy Fillings

Use these with Varenyky or Pyrohy.

Meat & Rice Filling

This is one of our favorite fillings. Give it a try and make it your own!

  • brown gravy packet
  • browned ground beef
  • cooked rice
  • frozen peas
  1. Make up the gravy packet, following the directions.
  2. Add ground beef, rice, and peas until everything is coated in gravy.
  3. Cool before using.

Potato Filling

  • 1-2 cups mashed potatoes (instant is fine)
  • 1/4 - 1/2 cup sharp cheese (Cheddar, farmer's cheese, etc.)
  1. Prepare the potatoes as you like. Don't add milk, because you want them on the dry side.
  2. Stir in your preferred cheese. Be careful not to add too much, because the potatoes will get gluey.
  3. Cool before using.

Sauerkraut Filling

  • 1 cup sauerkraut
  • bacon bits
  • salt & pepper
  • sour cream
  1. Rinse & drain the sauerkraut. If the pieces are on the long side, chop them lightly with a knife.
  2. Stir in the bacon bits, salt & pepper, and enough sour cream to moisten everything and keep it together.
